Here is a timeline of Niko Bellic's Life

  • Early Life:

    • Born in Eastern Europe to Milica Bellic and an abusive, alcoholic father.
    • Experienced a difficult childhood and endured various hardships.

  • Teenage Years:

    • Participated in an unnamed war as an infantryman, tank driver, and helicopter pilot.
    • Witnessed numerous atrocities, which led to a cynical worldview.
    • Escaped an ambush, concluding that his unit was betrayed by one of their own.

  • Post-War Struggles:

    • Struggled to adapt to civilian life; his brother was killed in action.
    • Joined the Balkanic criminal underworld, working for Ray Bulgarin.
    • Fled to the U.S. after being accused of sinking a smuggling ship.

  • 2008 – Arrival in Liberty City:

    • Joined cousin Roman, who was struggling with gambling debts and a failing taxi business.
    • Protected Roman from loan sharks and worked for Russian gangster Vlad Glebov.

  • Early Criminal Endeavors in Liberty City:

    • Killed Vlad Glebov, leading to employment with mob boss Mikhail Faustin.
    • Betrayed by Faustin’s deputy, Dimitri Rascalov, and targeted by Ray Bulgarin.
    • Moved to Bohan after Roman's apartment and cab depot were burned down.

  • Making New Alliances:

    • Formed alliances with Yardie second-in-command Little Jacob and Irish gangster Patrick McReary.
    • Forced to work for undercover agent Michelle (Karen) and the United Liberty Paper (ULP) agency.

  • Key Missions and Discoveries:

    • Assisted Ray Boccino with a diamond deal, discovering Florian Cravic's innocence.
    • Located the true traitor, Darko Brevic, with ULP’s help.

  • Final Confrontation:

    • Assisted Don Jimmy Pegorino with a heroin deal involving Dimitri, leading to a crucial choice.
    • Kills Dimitri, leading to Kate’s death and eventually kills Pegorino with help from Roman and Little Jacob.

  • Conclusion:

    • Niko learns the child will be named after Kate.
    • Niko reflects on the hollow nature of the American Dream, concluding it is a promise no one can truly achieve.
